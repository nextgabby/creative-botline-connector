Notification Products (THREE distinct products — never merge names or mechanics)

1. Scheduled Notification Program — the user opts in (typically by liking a CTA post), receives one instant notification confirming opt-in, and then all subsequent notifications are SCHEDULED at set times. This is not random, not sequential — it is scheduled. "Scheduled notifications" and "Scheduled Notification Program" refer to the same product. Does NOT require Creative Strategy support.
2. Sequential Instant Notifications (SIN) — the user likes a CTA and receives an instant notification, then keeps liking each notification to receive the next one, delivered IN SEQUENCE (1 → 2 → 3…). User-driven, ordered. Requires Creative Strategy support.
3. Randomized Instant Notifications (RIN) — the user likes a CTA and receives ONE RANDOM notification from a pool of options (e.g. 1 of 15). Not sequential, not scheduled — one random pick. Requires Creative Strategy support.

To drive meaningful opt-in for any notification product, always include a clear incentive such as exclusive discount codes, giveaways, or offers available only in the brand's app or at location.
Describe timing in general terms only (e.g., "notifications will begin sending at kickoff," "around peak hours," "timed closely to key moments"). Never imply exact or guaranteed timing control.
